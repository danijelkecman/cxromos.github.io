Aviation is not a good place for casual automation.

That does not mean AI has no role. It means AI has to be boxed into workflows where the system can expose sources, constraints, uncertainty, and responsibility.

Useful aviation copilots will not be generic chatbots. They will sit on top of structured operational data: routes, waypoints, weather, NOTAMs, aircraft constraints, documents, and live system events.

The design principle is simple: AI may assist reasoning, but the software must preserve traceability.

If the system cannot answer “why did you suggest this?” it is not operational software. It is theater.
