A route is not a line on a map. It is a sequence of decisions.

Graph-based route intelligence starts with nodes and edges, but the real value is in the constraints: proximity, direction, airways, restricted areas, weather, operational preferences, and fallback paths.

The goal is not merely to compute a route. The goal is to explain why one route is acceptable, why another is risky, and where the decision can fail.

That is where graph algorithms, geospatial systems, and AI-assisted interpretation start to become a real operational product.
